Minimalism in design isn't about stripping away features; it's about removing noise. When building user interfaces, every single line, border, and color change asks for the user's attention.

## Typography as Layout

One of the most effective ways to build a clean interface is to rely on typography for structure rather than boxes and borders. By adjusting font weights, sizes, and colors (using shades of gray), you can establish a clear visual hierarchy.

*   **Primary Elements:** High contrast, heavy font weights.
*   **Secondary Elements:** Muted colors, smaller sizes.
*   **Dividers:** Use ample whitespace instead of actual line dividers whenever possible.

## Dark Mode Considerations

A truly excellent dark mode isn't just an inverted light mode. It requires careful consideration of contrast. Pure black `#000000` is often too harsh; instead, using deep grays (like `#121215`) provides a much softer, more premium feel. 

When you combine clean typography with a well-considered color palette, the result is an interface that feels invisible, allowing the content itself to shine.
