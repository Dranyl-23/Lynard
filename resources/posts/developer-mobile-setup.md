If you check out my [/gear](/gear) page, you might notice that it's heavily skewed towards mobile tech—specifically, my Vivo V27 Pro and an Anker powerbank. 

As a web developer, it's easy to get obsessed with multi-monitor setups and mechanical keyboards. But the reality is that the vast majority of users will experience your software on a 6-inch glass screen while waiting in line for coffee.

## Designing Mobile-First

I rely heavily on my smartphone as a testing ground. Every time I build a new UI component in Tailwind CSS, I immediately open it on my Vivo V27 Pro. Testing on a physical device reveals issues that browser devtools simply can't simulate:

1.  **Touch Targets:** Are the buttons actually easy to tap with a thumb?
2.  **Scroll Momentum:** Does the page feel native, or is the scrolling janky?
3.  **Real-World Lighting:** Does the contrast hold up when I'm standing outside in the sun?

## The On-The-Go Workflow

Working remotely means I'm not always tethered to a desk. 

With cloud-based IDEs and platforms like GitHub Codespaces, I can review pull requests, deploy code to Vercel, and even write quick bug fixes directly from my phone. A high-capacity Anker powerbank and noise-canceling earbuds are my lifeline when working from airports or cafes.

The best setup isn't the most expensive one; it's the one that removes friction between you and shipping your code.
